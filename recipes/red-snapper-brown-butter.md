## Ingredients
- 3 red snapper fillets (skin-on)
- 4 tbsp lactose-free butter
- 2 tbsp capers (drained)
- 2 tbsp rice wine vinegar
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2 garlic cloves (thinly sliced)
- 2 tbsp fresh parsley (chopped)
- 1 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 1 lb russet potatoes
- 1/2 lb haricots verts (thin green beans)
- 2 tbsp olive oil (for potatoes)

## Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Peel and cut russet potatoes into 1-inch cubes. Soak in cold water for 15–20 minutes to remove excess starch, then drain and pat completely dry. Toss with olive oil, salt, and pepper and spread in a single layer on a baking sheet.
2. Roast potatoes for 35–40 minutes until edges are golden and crispy and a fork slides in easily, flipping halfway through.
3. Pat red snapper fillets completely dry with paper towels. Score the skin lightly with 2–3 diagonal cuts. Season both sides generously with salt and pepper.
4. Bring a pot of salted water to a boil. Blanch haricots verts for about 4 minutes until just tender but still bright green, then drain and set aside.
5. Heat olive oil in a large stainless steel or cast iron skillet over medium-high heat until shimmering. Place fillets skin-side down and press gently with a spatula for the first 30 seconds to prevent curling. Cook undisturbed for about 4–5 minutes until the skin is deeply golden and crispy and the flesh is cooked about 3/4 of the way up.
6. Flip fillets and cook the flesh side for just 1–2 more minutes until cooked through. Remove fish to a warm plate, skin-side up to keep it crispy.
7. Reduce heat to medium. Add lactose-free butter to the same pan. Swirl continuously until the butter turns golden amber and smells nutty. Add garlic and cook for 30 seconds, then add capers and rice wine vinegar. Stir to combine and remove from heat. Stir in parsley.
8. Arrange potatoes and haricots verts on each plate. Place a snapper fillet on top, skin-side up. Spoon the brown butter caper sauce generously over the fish and serve immediately.
